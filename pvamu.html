Why Mechanical Engineering?

It all started in the 9th grade. At my high school, it was required that every student took an elective course.

At first, I chose health, but quickly realized that it wasn't as interesting to me as I thought it would be. Soon after this realization, I switched to a course called Engineering & Design.

The main focus in this course was to design several components using a 3D CAD software called SolidWorks. Following this course, I decided to pursue this career in college.

Freshman Year

Freshman year was an absolute rollercoaster for me, and not in a good way. My priorities were completely out of place—I was hanging out with the wrong crowd and failing nearly all of my classes.

One class, in particular, stood out as my least favorite: an intro to coding course focused on C++. There were many reasons I struggled with it, but the biggest one was that I found coding incredibly difficult to understand. Looking back, it’s no surprise—I barely studied and wasn’t doing myself any favors.

I went into the class knowing nothing about coding, and as the semester progressed, my frustration only grew. Test after test, I failed miserably, to the point where I started to believe I might have to pack up, go home, and drop out of college altogether.

As time went on, I had countless conversations with my mom, who always reassured me that I could achieve anything I set my mind to. These talks became a turning point in my life, as they led me to a pivotal realization: I had complete control over the choices I made. It was both liberating and nerve-wracking to understand that my success—or failure—was entirely up to me.

With this new mindset, I decided to fully commit to all of my classes, even the coding course I had grown to despise. By the end of the semester, my hard work paid off—I finished that class with an A+.

Sophomore Year

Sophomore year marked a season of transformation. I was determined to turn my path around and become more involved—not just within my major, but also across various organizations on campus.

I kicked off the semester by joining the media team as a videographer for our marching band, combining my passion for videography and video editing with my desire to contribute to something meaningful. This opportunity allowed me to capture and celebrate the spirit of the band while doing something I truly loved.
